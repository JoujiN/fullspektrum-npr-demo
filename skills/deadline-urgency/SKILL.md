# Skill: Deadline Urgency

## What I Do

I calibrate formal correspondence based on deadline proximity and consequence. I keep language clear, factual, and accessible for neurodivergent adults: short paragraphs, explicit requests, labelled placeholders, and optional access-needs wording when the user wants it.

## Tone Tiers

### Tier 1 - Standard

Use when: first contact, proactive enquiry, or response comfortably within deadline.

Characteristics:

- polite and cooperative
- assumes good faith
- states the purpose clearly
- ends with a specific follow-up request

Opening pattern:

```text
I am writing to request clarification about...
```

### Tier 2 - Urgent

Use when: deadline is imminent, a previous contact was unanswered, or an automated payment failed.

Characteristics:

- firm and professional
- states the deadline and likely consequence
- requests a response in a short window
- signals escalation may be needed without threatening legal action

Opening pattern:

```text
I am writing as a matter of urgency regarding...
```

### Tier 3 - Escalation

Use when: deadline has passed, prior contacts were ignored, financial harm occurred, a formal complaint is needed, or a statutory/contractual right may be affected.

Characteristics:

- formal complaint register
- numbered chronology
- clear remedy requested
- short hard deadline before escalation to the appropriate complaint route
- no apologetic framing

Opening pattern:

```text
I write to make a formal complaint regarding...
```

## Reference Table

Use cautious wording such as "may be relevant" unless the source data is certain.

| Organisation type | Possible reference point | Escalation route |
|---|---|---|
| HMRC | Taxes Management Act 1970; Finance Acts | HMRC complaints, Adjudicator's Office, Parliamentary Ombudsman |
| Council tax | Local Government Finance Act 1992 | Council complaints procedure, Local Government Ombudsman |
| Energy supplier | Ofgem licence conditions | Supplier complaints, Energy Ombudsman |
| Water supplier | Water Industry Act 1991 | Supplier complaints, Consumer Council for Water |
| Financial institution | FCA rules; Consumer Credit Act 1974 where relevant | Financial Ombudsman Service |
| Landlord | Landlord and Tenant Act 1985; Housing Act 2004 where relevant | Council, tenancy advice, Housing Ombudsman |
| NHS | NHS complaints route; PALS | PALS, NHS complaints, Parliamentary and Health Service Ombudsman |
| Employer | Equality Act 2010 where adjustment/access needs are involved | Internal grievance, ACAS, legal advice |

Do not cite law as definitive unless the facts clearly support it. Prefer: "Please treat this as a formal complaint under your complaints procedure" when uncertain.

## Accessibility Paragraph

Use only when appropriate and user-consented:

```text
I would be grateful if you could communicate in writing where possible and set out any requested actions as clear numbered steps. This helps me respond accurately and on time.
```

Do not disclose disability, diagnosis, or neurodivergence unless the user explicitly asks for that disclosure.

## Letter Templates

### Standard enquiry / first contact

```text
[Your Name]
[Your Address]
[Date]

[Recipient Name or "The Complaints Team"]
[Organisation]
[Address]

Dear [Sir/Madam / Name],

Re: [Account / Reference / Case number if known]

I am writing to [state purpose].

[Short factual paragraph with dates, amounts, and references known from the task data.]

Please confirm receipt and let me know the next steps in writing within [14 / 21] days.

[Optional access-needs paragraph if user-consented.]

Yours faithfully,
[Name]
[Contact details]
```

### Urgent contact

```text
[Your Name]
[Your Address]
[Date]

[Recipient Name or "The Complaints Team"]
[Organisation]
[Address]

Dear [Sir/Madam / Name],

Re: [Account / Reference] - URGENT: response requested by [Date]

I am writing as a matter of urgency regarding [issue].

The deadline for [action] is [date]. If this is not resolved by then, [specific consequence if known].

I request a written response by [date], so there is enough time to act before the deadline.

[Optional short chronology.]

[Optional access-needs paragraph if user-consented.]

Yours faithfully,
[Name]
```

### Escalation / formal complaint

```text
[Your Name]
[Your Address]
[Date]

[Recipient Name or "The Complaints Team"]
[Organisation]
[Address]

Dear [Sir/Madam / Name],

FORMAL COMPLAINT - Re: [Account / Reference]

I write to make a formal complaint regarding [organisation]'s handling of [specific issue].

Chronology:
1. [Date]: [Action or event]
2. [Date]: [Action or event]
3. [Date]: [Action or event]

The remedy I request is:
- [Specific action]
- [Financial correction if known]
- Written confirmation by [date]

If this matter is not resolved by [date], I will consider escalating it through the relevant complaints or ombudsman route.

I attach: [list evidence or use "no attachments at this stage"].

[Optional access-needs paragraph if user-consented.]

Yours faithfully,
[Name]
```

## Rules

- Use British English.
- Prefer plain English over jargon.
- Keep paragraphs short.
- Use exact known facts.
- Label unknown facts with `[INSERT: field]`.
- Do not over-disclose personal, medical, disability, or financial data.
- Do not threaten action the user has not asked for or may not be able to take.
