# NPR Onboarding Conversation

Use this skill during the live onboarding conversation.

## Principles

- Ask one human question at a time.
- Keep each intake message under 80 words.
- Reflect the user's previous answer before asking the next question when that helps continuity.
- Prefer plain words over labels. Ask about "how you work best", not "traits" or "psychometrics".
- Treat short answers as valid, then ask a gentle follow-up if the needed signal is still weak.
- Do not mention stages, schemas, coordination keys, gates, agents, or internal validation.

## Question Shape

Good onboarding questions are:

- open enough to invite a real answer
- narrow enough that the user knows what to respond to
- grounded in what has already been said
- safe to answer without feeling assessed

When choosing the next question, target the current pending dimension and avoid collecting two dimensions in one turn.
